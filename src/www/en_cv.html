My Story

Commencing my developer journey in 2002, I worked daily with JavaScript, HTML and CSS – the foundational tools for shaping today's UIs. However, it wasn't until around 2016 that I fully embraced the transformative power of TypeScript (v2). While recognizing the groundbreaking nature of ES6, TypeScript's superior features have been my preference to this day.

Adoption of TypeScript played a pivotal role, transitioning me from a full-stack developer to a front-ender. My journey involved working on SPAs using jQuery initially, followed by exploring Vue.js (v1) and Angular (v2).

Shortly after the advent of Node.js and its package system, I embraced industry standards such as Prettier, ESLint (TSlint at the time) and Webpack to build bigger, better apps with greater confidence.

In 2019, the introduction of hooks to React caused me to jump at it. React's minimalist, functional programming approach led me to almost abandon OOP's classes. This preference for purity and minimalism is why I love React and TypeScript.

I also enjoy teaching. I engage in 1:1 private teaching occasionally and enjoy sharing knowledge as both a mentor and a mentee.

My Tech Stack

In crafting my tech stacks, React with TypeScript remains a constant, but for a new personal project, I might choose a Vite + React + TypeScript stack accompanied by tools like Prettier, ESLint, Tailwind, DaisyUI, TanStack Query, Axios / Ofetch, Preact Signals and possibly MobX or Redux.

For server-side rendered apps, I favor Vite's SSR plugin, although Next.js is a suitable alternative. Express.js serves as my go-to for small project APIs, while Nest.js is preferred for larger ones.

For CMS needs, I appreciate the simplicity, freedom, and Node compatibility of headless Strapi, but alternatives abound.

I'm not hesitant to utilize Electron for a dedicated app window and explore ReactNative / PWA for mobile solutions.

Test-driven development is a crucial practice, and I have a long-term appreciation for Jest and Playwright.

Preferences of course vary based on the application's scale, expected longevity, budget and other factors, adapting to the unique requirements of each project.
